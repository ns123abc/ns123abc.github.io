Book Reviews, Briefly:

  • Fyodor Doestoevsky, Crime & Punishment

    • 5.0/5.0 - Literary perfection. Gripping and suspenseful. Disturbing and captivating. A psychological rabbit hole that explores the human condition through the lens of Raskolnikov on topics of morality, crime, guilt, remorse, and madness. Dostoevsky masterfully weaves together complex plots with depth of psychological states of its characters in this thriller. A very absorbing read.

  • Peter Zeihan, The End of the World Is Just the Beginning

    • 4.0/5.0 - Thought-provoking, humurous and straight forward analysis that integrates the effects on nations of geography, resources, demographics and economics to explain the history, understand the current situation and forecast the future. I liked Zehian's interviews more than the book, still found it useful, thought i wished for more information on India.
