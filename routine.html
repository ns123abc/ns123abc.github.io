Notes on Routine and Habits.

A typical day usually begins at around 10:30.

I tend to try and keep my eyes shut until I have mentally drafted a rough plan of the things I want to have done by the end of that day. I rise to find the biggest glass to drink water, give a kiss and hug to a family memeber and go outside to meet my cats and touch grass.

My goal for each day , is to complete a single task that should take about 6 hours to complete.

I immediately set off to complete the talk i planned in bed. I know i have about 3 hours of undistrubed flow before the distractions pull me away.

After about 3 hours, I usually cook for a half-hour, and then eat for a half-hour, and then walk for a half-hour, to fully leave the haze of the flowstate.

The afternoon is spent doing maintenance, superficial work and experiments. But mostly, the afternoon is spent reading and learning. The goal is to build a catalog of exciting things to wake up to the next day and to experiment with.

The day ends with journaling for a half-hour at which point I record the task done, and the lessons learned . Before sleeping, I usually read for a half-hour , I go to bed with a book , and a highlighter pen. I overline the things I want to keep for a later use, or to revisit.
